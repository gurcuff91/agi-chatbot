**What must be considered?**
- Each piece can measure up to 62 linear inches (158 cm) total (height + length + width).
- Pieces whose combined linear measurements are between 63 inches (159 cm) and 107 inches (272 cm) are considered excess baggage.
- Pieces whose combined linear measurements exceed 115 inches (294 cm) will not be accepted as baggage and must be transported as cargo.
- If a piece has wheels, they are not included in the linear measurements.

**What are linear measurements?** It is the result of the sum of height + length + width of the luggage.

##### 1.1.1.8 Baggage Policy
The baggage policy that applies for the trip depends on the flight date, origin and destination, fare class and PreferMember status.

**Weight**
- Weight and pieces allowed are per passenger and are not transferable to other passengers.

**Taxes**
- Baggage charges are subject to the taxes established in the country of origin.

**Extra or overweight baggage**
- Baggage charges are subject to taxes established in the country of origin.

**Restrictions**
- There may be baggage restrictions depending on the time of year and the origin and destination.
- Certain items require a signed liability document in order to be transported.
- The registration of pieces or packages with an irregular shape and/or size is not allowed on flights to and from Cuba, Ecuador and El Salvador.

**Carry-on**
- Carry-on baggage items that do not comply with the policies and rules of the ticket and are brought to the gate will be checked for hold and charged as additional baggage.
- If the passenger refuses or cannot make the payment, Copa Airlines will not be responsible for the baggage and its transport and will remain in custody by the airport authorities.

**Separate reservations**
- If there are separate reservations, the luggage will be registered to the destination of the first reservation with which the trip begins. This means that at the connection point the baggage must be collected and checked in with the connecting airline.

#### 1.1.4 Carry-on
Information to prepare luggage and items before traveling.

- Source URL: https://www.copaair.com/en-us/travel-information/baggage-information/carry-on/

##### 1.1.4.1 What is considered hand luggage?
Any item that the passenger carries with them in the cabin and that can be easily placed in the overhead bin or under the front seat.

**1 Carry-on**  
Dimensions: 22" high x 14" long x 10" wide (56 cm high x 36 cm long x 26 cm wide)

**1 Personal item**  
Dimensions: 17" high x 10" long x 9" wide (43 cm high x 25 cm long x 22 cm wide)

##### 1.1.4.2 Carry-on policy
Each passenger has the right to carry one (1) carry-on, which must be able to be easily placed in the overhead compartment.

In our effort to minimize physical interactions, we ask that your carry-on bag be light enough that you can lift and store it yourself in the overhead bin, without assistance.

**Applies to all passengers, including Star Alliance Gold members.**

###### 1.1.4.2.1 What must be considered?
- Each piece of carry-on baggage can measure up to 46 linear inches (118 cm): 22" high x 14" long x 10" wide (56 cm high x 36 cm long x 26 cm wide). This includes handles and wheels.
- All passengers can carry a hand luggage weighing up to 10 Kg (22 lbs).
- Any piece of carry-on baggage that is brought to the boarding gate in excess of the baggage policy that applies according to the rules of your ticket will be registered to carry in the hold and charged as additional baggage. In the event that the passenger refuses or is unable to pay for the additional baggage, Copa Airlines will not be responsible for the baggage, nor for the transport of the luggage abandoned at the gate, given that they will be guarded by the airport authorities.

**What are linear measurements?** It is the result of the sum of height x length x width of your luggage.

##### 1.1.4.3 Personal item
Each passenger may carry one (1) personal item in addition to the carry-on baggage allowance. This item must fit under the front seat.

**We consider the following as a personal item:**
- A handbag / personal purse
- A backpack
- A laptop bag or case

###### 1.1.4.3.1 What must be considered?
- Each piece can measure up to 36 linear inches (90 cm): 17" high x 10" long x 9" wide (43 cm high x 25 cm long x 22 cm wide).
- If the personal item exceeds the permitted dimensions, it will be sent to the aircraft hold as checked baggage and applicable excess baggage charges will apply.

**What are linear measurements?** It is the result of the sum of height x length x width of your luggage.

##### 1.1.4.4 Additional personal items allowed on board
You can also bring on board the following personal items as long as you can stow them under the seat in front of you:
- A coat or jacket. If it is a bulky garment, it will be considered a personal item or must be placed inside the carry-on baggage.
- Food for consumption on the aircraft.
- Products purchased at the airport that do not exceed the dimensions of the allowed personal item.

##### 1.1.4.5 Other special items
Stroller* and other infant items (if traveling with an infant aged 0 to 23 months), one (1) of the following four (4) items applies:
- A infant seat, if the infant has a purchased seat.
- A small folding stroller for infant.
- Large infant cars must be registered at the counters so that they can be transported as baggage in the aircraft hold.
- A diaper bag that complies with the permitted dimensions of the personal item and fits under the seat in front of the responsible adult.

\*The baby stroller must not exceed 110 cm in length; any stroller exceeding this limit must be checked at the counter.

**Important:** Children (2 years and older) may travel with a stroller, which will replace the allowed carry-on item, provided it meets the established carry-on size requirements.

Learn more about traveling with infants: travel with infants

Many commonly used items can be very dangerous in flight. Variations in temperature and pressure can cause certain products to spill, generate toxic gases, or start fires.

Carry-on: Not allowed  
Checked baggage: Allowed

**Firearms**

- Any foreign passenger bound for Guatemala who requires the entry of firearms for civil and/or sporting use must submit a request to the Guatemalan Consulate so that the General Directorate for the Control of Arms and Ammunition (DIGECAM) issues an authorization and import license or special and temporary license to carry and/or transfer to enter the weapon into the country. This request must be made in advance of the travel date.  
- All passengers bound for Colombia who require the entry of firearms for civilian and/or sporting use must transport all firearms in containers separate from hold baggage (case or safety boxes), duly closed, with a security system (padlock or key), identification system (bag tag) and the corresponding security declarations.  

Carry-on: Not allowed  
Checked baggage: Allowed  
The passenger must declare their intention to transport a firearm in their checked baggage, before the security question. For this you need a permit to enter the weapon in the country of your destination.  
The passenger must present the appropriate documentation in order to verify that the weapon is legal.  
In the case of firearms such as shotgun or rifle, the luggage that transports them must be properly closed. Only the passenger who will check the baggage will have the key or the combination of the piece of baggage.

**Disabling elements**

**Ammunition (cartridges for weapons)**  
Securely packed (of Division 1.4S UN 0012 or UN 0014 only), in quantities not exceeding 5 kilograms of gross weight per person for that person's use.

Carry-on: Not allowed

###### 1.1.5.1.5 Batteries

**Spare lithium batteries**  
- They are not allowed in your checked baggage.  
- In order to be transported, they must be packed in sealed bags that do not allow the release of substances and carried in ICOPOR or plastic coolers, well secured and closed so as not to allow the release of any residue regardless of the position of the packaging. Some foods are restricted in some countries; you should verify that they are not subject to governmental laws.  

Carry-on: Read the instructions 1 and 2 below
1. Spare/loose lithium battery should be packaged as follows to prevent short circuit:  
- In its original packaging, protective packaging or in a resealable plastic bag (one battery per bag).  
- Placing masking tape over the exposed battery terminals.  
- When placing batteries in your carry-on baggage, make sure they do not come into contact with metallic objects, such as coins, keys or jewelry.  
- Prevent batteries from being crushed or punctured by other objects inside the luggage.
1. Size and quantity limits:  
- Lithium metal battery (non-rechargeable):  
  - 2 grams to 8 grams per battery.  
  - Maximum 20 batteries per passenger.  
- Lithium ion battery (rechargeable):  
  - Capacity from 100 Wh to 160 Wh per battery.  
  - Two spare batteries in carry-on baggage.  
  - Only 15 electronic devices are allowed per person, with the appropriate battery installed.  

Checked baggage: Not allowed  

**Wheelchair batteries**  
You are allowed to carry wheelchair batteries in carry-on or checked baggage as long as they meet the specific requirements described below.  
- In order to be transported, they must be packed in sealed bags that do not allow the release of substances and carried in ICOPOR or plastic refrigerators, well secured and closed that do not allow the release of any residue regardless of the position in which the packaging is located. Some foods are restricted in some countries; You must verify that they are not subject to government laws.  

Carry-on: Read the instruction 1 below
1. Lithium batteries:  
- The battery should be removed from the equipment and placed in a protective case.  
- Battery and spare must not exceed 300 Wh. For equipment that requires two batteries, each battery must not exceed 160 Wh.
  
Checked baggage: Read the instructions 1 and 2 below
1. Non-spillable batteries:  
- They can be transported as long as the edges are disconnected and insulated and the battery is properly secured to the wheelchair.  

2. Spillable batteries:  
- The edges must be disconnected and insulated, the battery secured to the wheelchair and the wheelchair placed upright on the aircraft, equally well secured.  
- If this is not possible, the wheelchair battery must be lowered (the wheelchair will then travel without problem), pack it in a strong metal or plastic packaging with compatible absorbent material and enough to absorb all the liquid, mark it with the word “wet battery with wheelchair” and with the corrosive label.  
- Finally, it will be raised ensuring that it is in a vertical position.  

**Wet batteries (spillable)**  
Exception: wheelchairs operated with these batteries.  

Carry-on: Not allowed  
Checked baggage: Not allowed  

**Smart suitcases with lithium batteries**  
Carry-on:  
- The battery must be removed from the suitcase and placed in a protective case during the flight. Smart bags with a fixed lithium battery are not allowed.  

Checked baggage:  
- The battery must be removed from the suitcase and transported on board, in carry-on baggage. Smart bags with a fixed lithium battery are not allowed.  

Passengers must have the technical specifications of their mobilization equipment (wheelchairs, walkers), so that airline personnel can corroborate the correct disconnection of the equipment.  

At all times the passenger is responsible for the disconnection of their equipment, the airline staff will only verify that the wheelchairs are properly turned off or disconnected.

###### 1.1.6.4.2 Dry ice
If the item in question is packed in dry ice, please make sure that the following conditions are met:
- The perishable items packed in containers with dry ice may not exceed the maximum weight allowed: 2 kg (4.4 lb) per carry-on luggage.
- The containers should be sturdy, vented and made of plastic.
- Containers made of polystyrene foam or styrofoam are not permitted for the transport of dry ice.
- If the perishable item is wrapped in cloth bags, sufficient plastic wrap must also be used to ensure that it does not leak (perishable items that are not wrapped in plastic will not be accepted).
- Dry ice cannot be placed, under any circumstances, in the same compartment or cargo hold with live animals.
- If the item is not packed in dry ice and might leak condensate fluids that could contaminate, soil and/or otherwise damage the cargo hold, the baggage contained in the hold or the cargo itself (e.g., fish, fruit, cooking oils, etc.), the item must be packed in one of the following manners before it will be accepted for transport:
  - The products must be wrapped in sturdy plastic and placed in plastic or styrofoam coolers. The cooler then also must be wrapped in plastic.
  - The products must be wrapped in plastic, placed in a cardboard box, and the box itself then wrapped in plastic.

Note: The plastic used must be sufficiently waterproof to prevent leaks. In addition, no fluid should leak outside the inner wrapping.

Copa Airlines is not responsible for the disappearance or loss of the following items:

Other considerations:
- Copa Airlines' liability is regulated by the Montreal Convention and the Warsaw Convention, so we recommend that the insurance company be notified of any irregularities occurring to the baggage. If insurance was purchased prior to travel, they will handle this issue with the airline.
- For international travel where the Warsaw Convention applies, Copa Airlines' liability is limited to a maximum of CHF 250 per kilogram and CHF 5000 per customer for unchecked baggage.
- For international travel to which the Montreal Convention applies (including domestic segments of international travel), Copa Airlines' liability is limited to 1,000 SDRs (Special Drawing Rights) per customer for checked and unchecked baggage.

#### 1.1.8 Three tips when checking baggage
Recommendations for your next flight.

1. **Prepay your baggage at the Web Check-In**  
   Do it 24 hours before your trip and save money and time at the airport.

2. **Register your baggage on time**  
   Register it up to 4 hours before the departure of the flight.

3. **Identify your baggage**  
   Put your full name, address and telephone number so that it is not confused with someone else's.
